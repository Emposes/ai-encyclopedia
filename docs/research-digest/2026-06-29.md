## What's new this week

### Landmark safety document: Singapore Consensus (arXiv:2506.20702)

Published this week. Yoshua Bengio and 100+ co-authors, building on the International AI
Safety Report 2026 (arXiv:2602.21012), produced the "Singapore Consensus on Global AI Safety
Research Priorities." It organises AI safety research into a Defence-in-Depth model covering
Development, Assessment, and Monitoring/Intervention challenges. Explicitly elevates scalable
oversight and AI debate to "paramount" priority. Backed by the SCAI conference (Singapore,
April 2025) involving 11 countries. This is the highest-profile international scientific
statement on AI safety priorities yet produced.

**Coverage check:** Not covered. Closest content: openmodels/05 (red-teaming), chapters/05 §8
(Constitutional AI), chapters/13 (mech. interp.). No chapter covers alignment theory,
scalable oversight, or AI control as a coherent topic. → **Proposed** (see issue).

### DeepMind AI Control Roadmap (June 2026)

Google DeepMind published a blog post and associated research on an internal "AI Control
Roadmap" for securing systems against increasingly capable and imperfectly aligned AI agents.
Covers trusted monitoring, sandbagging detection, and graduated autonomy.

**Coverage check:** Same gap as above — subsumed into the AI Safety proposal.

### q-fin sweep

Papers this week include LLM financial forecasters with reflexivity prompting, hybrid
sentiment+ensemble approaches, and Bitcoin trading ML under transaction costs. No single paper
represents a new technique warranting a new chapter — all are applications of methods already
covered (ensembles: ml/14, LLMs as forecasters: cross-reference timeseries/06).

**Coverage check:** Adequate for now. Monitor for causal inference in finance (could eventually
justify a section in a Causal ML chapter).

### Causal ML survey v3 (May 2026 update)

Kaddour et al. "Causal Machine Learning: A Survey and Open Problems" reached v3 in May 2026,
with expanded coverage of causal representation learning and LLM-powered causal agents
(Causal-Copilot, arXiv:2504.13263, April 2026). Double/Debiased ML (Chernozhukov et al.)
continues to be adopted in industry for A/B test analysis at scale.

**Coverage check:** stats/03 §5 mentions the do-operator and DAGs in two paragraphs. No chapter
covers CATE estimation, causal forests, or Double ML. → **Proposed** (see issue).

### AI for Science consolidation

AlphaFold3 (Evans et al., Nature 2024), Isomorphic Labs' IsoDDE (Feb 2026), and GNoME
(Merchant et al., Nature 2023) collectively represent a Nobel-Prize-winning paradigm shift in
scientific ML. The field is consolidating around equivariant networks and diffusion for
molecules. Survey papers (arXiv:2503.08979, arXiv:2508.14111) provide comprehensive coverage
of agentic scientific discovery.

**Coverage check:** No chapter in the encyclopedia covers scientific ML as a domain. The
multimodal track (vision, audio, generation, speech, world models, robotics) has no chapter
on molecular/structural biology data types or equivariant architectures. → **Proposed** (see issue).

### MLEvolve / automated ML algorithm discovery (arXiv:2606.06473)

Self-evolving framework that uses LLMs to discover new ML optimisers and architectures. Novel
and interesting but too early-stage to warrant a chapter — single paper, not yet peer-reviewed
at a top venue, methods unstable.

**Dismissed:** Not mature enough. Revisit in 3–6 months.

### Formal theorem proving (Goedel-Architect, arXiv:2606.06468)

Streamlines formal theorem proving with LLM-based proof search. Interesting intersection of
neuro-symbolic and LLM research, but too domain-specific for a general encyclopedia chapter.

**Dismissed:** Too niche. The topic of formal verification could eventually warrant a section
in a Neuro-Symbolic chapter, but the base concept doesn't yet have a proposal.

### Test-Time Training / Adaptation (multiple papers)

EASE-TTT (arXiv:2606.06906, June 2026), Test-Time Learning for LLMs (arXiv:2505.20633), and
related papers show growing interest in gradient-based adaptation at inference time. This is
genuinely distinct from inference-time compute scaling (chapters/12), which uses fixed weights.

**Coverage check:** Not explicitly covered. However, as a single technique rather than a full
paradigm, it likely belongs as a new section in chapters/12 rather than a standalone chapter.

**Disposition:** Not proposed this week. Recommend monitoring for 4–6 more weeks to assess
whether TTT is converging to a stable set of methods worth a standalone chapter.

### Causal-Copilot / LLM-Powered Causal Analysis (arXiv:2504.13263)

LLM agent framework for end-to-end causal analysis (discovery → identification → estimation).
Subsumed into the Causal ML proposal as a "Why now" signal.
