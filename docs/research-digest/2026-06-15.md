## What's new this week

### arXiv / recent papers

- **Flow matching adoption**: NeurIPS 2025 closed with 30+ accepted FM papers; ICLR 2026 received 150+ FM-related submissions. Rectified flow (Liu et al. arXiv:2209.03003) is now the default training objective for image (FLUX, SD3) and video generation.
- **MoE inference efficiency**: Multiple 2026 papers address MoE inference at scale — expert streaming (arXiv:2603.27624), SiftMoE similarity-aware expert selection (arXiv:2603.23888), memory-efficient MoE fine-tuning (arXiv:2602.11192). The theme: the deployment gap between MoE and dense models is closing fast.
- **Synthetic data scaling laws**: Busbridge et al. (arXiv:2503.19551) establish that synthetic data follows different scaling laws from natural corpora; Kang et al. (arXiv:2510.01631) show pre-training on diverse synthetic mixtures outperforms natural-only baselines in some regimes.
- **Test-time training**: Multiple 2025-2026 papers (arXiv:2503.11842, arXiv:2602.21204, arXiv:2606.06906) explore TTT for long-context adaptation and in-context learning improvement. Promising but the field is still settling — deferred to next run.
- **LLM Benchmark Methodology**: "Benchmark Health Index" (arXiv:2602.11674) provides a framework for evaluating benchmark quality itself; benchmark contamination detection papers continue accumulating.
- **GNNs**: Active surveys in drug discovery (arXiv:2506.06915) and financial markets (arXiv:2410.16858). No new architectural breakthrough — GNNs remain mature and under-documented in the encyclopedia.
- **Continual learning for LLMs**: CSUR 2025 comprehensive survey (arXiv:2404.16789, ACM dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3735633) now published. Mechanistic analysis of forgetting in LLMs (arXiv:2601.18699) appeared Jan 2026.
- **KAN (Kolmogorov-Arnold Networks)**: Comprehensive survey at v7+ (arXiv:2407.11075). Specialised variants (Temporal-KAN, FastKAN) are published. Adoption in production remains limited.
- **Agentic LLMs for quant finance**: "Agentic Trading" (arXiv:2605.19337), "QuantaAlpha" (arXiv:2602.07085), ICLR 2026 paper on rationality-driven trading agents. Active area but evidence on long-run market outperformance is contested (KDD2026 paper).

### Lab & industry news

- Anthropic Claude Opus 4.8 (May 2026) introduced dynamic multi-agent orchestration as a production feature.
- NVIDIA Cosmos 3 and Intel Xeon 6+ deliver MoE-optimised inference throughput gains.
- GTC 2026 was dominated by agentic deployments in Fortune 500 manufacturing and logistics (Foxconn MoMClaw: 80% RCA time reduction).
- Mechanistic interpretability named a 2026 MIT Technology Review Breakthrough Technology; Anthropic circuit-tracer and DeepMind Gemma Scope 2 released as open tools.
