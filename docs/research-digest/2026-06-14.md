## Proposed (4 new chapters)

### 1. Inference-Time Scaling
**Stub:** `docs/research-queue/inference-time-scaling.md`  
**Proposed as:** `chapters/12-inference-time-scaling.html` (advanced)

The o1/DeepSeek-R1/s1 wave established inference-time compute as a second scaling axis, independent of training-compute. Multiple independent groups have now reproduced test-time scaling curves, documented the "overthinking" failure mode, and derived preliminary compute-optimal inference laws (Snell et al. 2024, arXiv:2408.03314). The post-training chapter notes reasoning models; the RL chapter covers GRPO; neither addresses the *inference budget* question directly. High durability — this is now foundational.

**Key references:** arXiv:2408.03314 · arXiv:2501.19393 · arXiv:2501.12948 · arXiv:2604.10739

### 2. Mechanistic Interpretability & Sparse Autoencoders
**Stub:** `docs/research-queue/mechanistic-interpretability.md`  
**Proposed as:** `chapters/13-mechanistic-interpretability.html` (advanced)

Anthropic's "Scaling Monosemanticity" (2024) demonstrated sparse autoencoders (SAEs) extract interpretable monosemantic features from Claude 3 Sonnet at scale. Independent confirmations from OpenAI and academic labs followed; a systematic survey appeared in March 2025 (arXiv:2503.05613). The existing `mlops/06-explainability` chapter covers SHAP/LIME (prediction-level); mechanistic interpretability — working at the level of weights, circuits, and residual-stream directions — is completely absent from the encyclopedia. Highly durable: cited in EU AI Act compliance contexts and core to alignment research.

**Key references:** transformer-circuits.pub/2021/framework · arXiv:2309.08600 · transformer-circuits.pub/2024/scaling-monosemanticity · arXiv:2503.05613

### 3. Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)
**Stub:** `docs/research-queue/retrieval-augmented-generation.md`  
**Proposed as:** `agents/07-retrieval-augmented-generation.html` (core)

RAG is the default grounding mechanism for knowledge-intensive LLM applications and has been standard since Lewis et al. (2020). The agentic RAG survey (arXiv:2501.09136) and a comprehensive architecture survey (arXiv:2506.00054) confirm the field has stabilized. `agents/02-context-engineering` mentions retrieval as one choice in context budget decisions, but the retrieval engineering stack itself (dense retrieval, chunking, reranking, hybrid search, faithfulness evaluation) has no home in the encyclopedia. Practical, durable, high reader demand.

**Key references:** arXiv:2005.11401 · arXiv:2004.04906 · arXiv:2310.11511 · arXiv:2309.15217 · arXiv:2506.00054

### 4. Model Merging & Task Vectors
**Stub:** `docs/research-queue/model-merging.md`  
**Proposed as:** `openmodels/06-model-merging.html` (advanced)

Task arithmetic (Ilharco et al., ICLR 2023, arXiv:2212.04089) showed that fine-tuned weight deltas compose linearly. TIES-Merging (NeurIPS 2023) and DARE (2024) extended this to multi-model merging with interference control. MergeKit (arXiv:2403.13257) industrialized the workflow; an ACM Computing Surveys taxonomy paper appeared in 2025 (arXiv:2603.09938). The `openmodels` track covers running, fine-tuning, and training open models — model merging is a fourth adaptation method with no chapter.

**Key references:** arXiv:2212.04089 · arXiv:2306.01708 · arXiv:2311.03099 · arXiv:2403.13257 · arXiv:2603.09938

## Factual updates flagged (no new chapter needed — small PR tier)

### A. `chapters/10-diffusion.html` — Section 4 (Flow Matching)
Flow matching is now the dominant training framework for image and video generation: Stable Diffusion 3, FLUX, Sora-class video models. The 2025–26 literature adds Rectified MeanFlow and MeanFlow for efficient one-step generation (arXiv:2511.23342). Section 4 likely warrants expansion from "brief mention" to a full section covering the interpolant construction, the straight-flow advantage, and the connection to score matching.

### B. `chapters/09-frontier.html` — Section 1 (Mixture-of-Experts)
2025–26 brought new comprehensive MoE scaling laws covering activation ratio, expert granularity, and shared-expert ratio as jointly optimized dimensions (arXiv:2509.23678, arXiv:2507.17702). The frontier chapter's MoE section should reference these and update the "efficiency leverage" framing.
